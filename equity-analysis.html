üìö Investor's Guide: How to Use These Metrics ‚ñº

üí∞ Valuation Metrics - "Is the stock cheap or expensive?"

P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings) <15 Good 15-25 Fair >25 Expensive
Price per share divided by earnings per share. Lower P/E suggests the stock is undervalued relative to its earnings. Compare with industry peers and historical averages.
üí° Usage: Look for P/E below industry average. A P/E of 4x means you pay $4 for every $1 of annual earnings.
P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book) <1 Undervalued 1-3 Fair >3 Expensive
Market price vs. book value (assets minus liabilities). P/B <1 means the stock trades below its net asset value - potential bargain.
üí° Usage: Best for asset-heavy companies. P/B <1 with profitable business = strong buy signal.
PEG Ratio (Price/Earnings-to-Growth) <1 Undervalued 1-2 Fair >2 Overvalued
P/E divided by earnings growth rate. Accounts for growth - a high P/E may be justified if growth is high. PEG <1 indicates undervaluation relative to growth.
üí° Usage: Compare companies with different growth rates. Alibaba PEG=0.01 means extremely undervalued given its growth.

üè• Financial Health - "Can the company survive and thrive?"

ROE (Return on Equity) >15% Excellent 10-15% Good <10% Poor
Net income as percentage of shareholder equity. Measures how efficiently the company uses investor money. Higher = better management efficiency.
üí° Usage: Look for consistent ROE >15%. Compare with competitors in same industry.
D/E Ratio (Debt-to-Equity) <0.5 Low Risk 0.5-1 Moderate >1 High Risk
Total debt divided by shareholder equity. Lower is generally safer. High D/E means the company relies heavily on borrowed money.
üí° Usage: Avoid companies with D/E >2 in volatile industries. Tech companies typically have low D/E.
FCF (Free Cash Flow) Positive & Growing Negative
Cash generated after capital expenditures. The "real" money available for dividends, buybacks, acquisitions, or debt repayment. More important than net income.
üí° Usage: FCF should be positive and growing. FCF/Market Cap = FCF Yield (compare to bond yields).

üìä Technical Indicators - "What does the price action say?"

RSI (Relative Strength Index, 0-100) <30 Oversold (Buy) 30-70 Neutral >70 Overbought (Sell)
Momentum oscillator measuring speed and change of price movements. RSI <30 suggests the stock is oversold and may bounce back. RSI >70 suggests overbought conditions.
üí° Usage: Buy when RSI <30 in an uptrend. Sell/avoid when RSI >70. Don't use alone - combine with trend analysis.
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) Positive & Rising = Bullish Negative & Falling = Bearish
Shows relationship between two moving averages. MACD above signal line = bullish. MACD below signal line = bearish. Crossovers indicate trend changes.
üí° Usage: Buy on bullish crossover (MACD crosses above signal). Sell on bearish crossover.
SMA 50/200 (Simple Moving Average) Price > SMA50 > SMA200 = Uptrend Price < SMA50 < SMA200 = Downtrend
Average price over 50 or 200 trading days. "Golden Cross" (SMA50 crosses above SMA200) = strong buy signal. "Death Cross" = strong sell signal.
üí° Usage: Buy when price bounces off SMA50 in uptrend. Use SMA200 as major support/resistance level.
52-Week Position (0-100%) <20% Near Low (Buy Opportunity) 20-80% Mid-Range >80% Near High (Caution)
Where current price sits within 52-week range. 0% = at 52-week low. 100% = at 52-week high. Helps identify entry points.
üí° Usage: Stocks near 52-week lows with good fundamentals may be undervalued. Be cautious near 52-week highs.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Metrics - "How risky is this investment?"

Beta (Volatility vs Market) <0.5 Low Volatility 0.5-1.2 Normal >1.5 High Volatility
Measures stock's volatility relative to market (Beta=1). Beta <1 = less volatile than market. Beta >1 = more volatile. High beta = bigger swings.
üí° Usage: Conservative investors prefer Beta <1. Aggressive investors may seek Beta >1 for higher potential returns.
Volatility (Annualized %) <25% Low 25-40% Moderate >40% High
Standard deviation of returns, annualized. Higher volatility = wider price swings = more risk but potentially more reward.
üí° Usage: High volatility stocks need wider stop-losses. Consider position sizing based on volatility.

üü¢ Strong Buy Signals
‚Ä¢ P/E & P/B below industry average ‚Ä¢ PEG < 1 ‚Ä¢ ROE > 15% ‚Ä¢ Low debt (D/E < 0.5) ‚Ä¢ Positive & growing FCF ‚Ä¢ RSI < 35 (oversold) ‚Ä¢ Price above SMA50
üî¥ Warning Signs
‚Ä¢ P/E > 30 with slowing growth ‚Ä¢ Declining ROE ‚Ä¢ High debt (D/E > 1) ‚Ä¢ Negative FCF ‚Ä¢ RSI > 70 (overbought) ‚Ä¢ Price below SMA200
‚è∞ When to Buy
1. Fundamentals are strong (low P/E, high ROE, positive FCF)
2. Technicals confirm (RSI oversold, MACD bullish crossover)
3. Price near support levels or 52-week low
4. Market sentiment is negative (contrarian opportunity)
üö™ When to Sell
1. Valuation becomes excessive (P/E > industry + 50%)
2. Fundamentals deteriorate (declining ROE, negative FCF)
3. Technical breakdown (price below SMA200)
4. Better opportunities elsewhere
